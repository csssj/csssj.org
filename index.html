Welcome, Visitors!

The Cactus and Succulent Society of San Jose (CSSSJ) welcomes you to our website.

The CSSSJ is a diverse group of people with a mutual interest in and fascination for cacti and other succulent plants.

Succulent plants are among the most bizarre, beautiful, and varied in the entire plant kingdom. If you share our interest in these fascinating and unique plants or just want to learn more, we invite you to join us at our meetings and have some fun.

Upcoming Events

Monthly Club Meeting: Sunday

February 2nd, 2020, 12:30 PM: Grafting

Have you ever wanted to learn how to make a cactus Frankenstein through grafting? Come to this month’s meeting for a lesson on how to graft a small cutting of a slow-growing cactus to a fast-growing root stock and increase the growth rate by 100x. Learning this skill allows you to quickly propagate rare and unusual varieties or save a beloved rotting plant before it’s too late. Club member Andrew Hazlett will present this month’s meeting on learning what it takes to graft cactus successfully.

Please note that February’s meeting will be shorter than usual, to prevent overlapping with the Superbowl. There will consequently be no mini-show. Mini-shows will resume in March.

Meetings take place at 12:30 PM in the Meeting Hall of the Emma Prusch Farm Park.

Directions: Park in the lot just off of 647 S King Road. As you enter the park itself, you’ll see the slightly elevated structure of the Meeting Hall off to the left of the entry roundabout.

Society Day at Ruth Bancroft Garden

Saturday Feb 29, 2020: 10am–4pm

This will be a joint field trip, along with members of the San Francisco Succulent and Cactus Society, to the Ruth Bancroft Garden in Walnut Creek.

The Featured Speaker will be Ron Parker, author of Chasing Centuries: The Search for Ancient Agave Cultivars

There will also be garden tours from the garden staff, including:

  • Aloes in Bloom with Brian Kemble (Curator of the Ruth Bancroft Gardens).
  • Ethnobotany with Ersten Imaoka.
  • General-interest tours with Stephen Lysaght.

2020 members of the San Francisco and San Jose societies may attend for $10.

Each attendee will able to attend the main presentation, an indoor slideshow, along with at least one garden tour. Additional tours will be available as space allows.

There will be ample time for individual exploration, shopping in the nursery, and socializing with members from the other society.

Please carpool. The Garden has very limited parking: we will be renting a lot with 50 spaces. Parking for single-occupant vehicles will be $10, but free for carpool vehicles. (This is what they call “incentivizing”.)

Food won’t be served on-site, so please bring your own brown-bag lunch.
