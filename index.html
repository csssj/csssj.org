Welcome, Visitors!

The Cactus and Succulent Society of San Jose (CSSSJ) welcomes you to our website.

The CSSSJ is a diverse group of people with a mutual interest in and fascination for cacti and other succulent plants.

Succulent plants are among the most bizarre, beautiful, and varied in the entire plant kingdom. If you share our interest in these fascinating and unique plants, or just want to learn more, we invite you to join us at our meetings and have some fun.

If you’d like to become a member, you can do so for $15 a person, or $20 a couple. See the Membership Page for additional details.

Upcoming Events

Club Meeting

Sunday, July 9th: Country Store!

Emma Prusch Farm Park

If you have been to our Country Store in the past, you know this is one of the most fun events of the season. It is a great opportunity for you to share your bounty with your fellow members. Bring items that you think other members would enjoy. If you have a loud voice, we would appreciate your help in calling out the numbers at each of the tables!

Special Note: this will take place at the Meeting Hall, which has limited space: if you plan to attend, please let us know by emailing your name(s) and phone number to: programs@csssj.org

The rules are as follows:

  • Separate tables will be set up for each category (cacti, succulents, misc. items (books, pots, rocks, etc), and desserts.
  • People place their items on the corresponding table. If you bring desserts, make certain they are individually wrapped.
  • Tickets will be available for purchase as 5 for $1: you decide how many you would like to buy. These are two-part tickets, and you place the first part in the dedicated pot. Because the numbers can be hard to hear, please write a reasonably unique combination of your first name, last name, or initials onto each ticket before placing it in the pot.
  • You now have one remaining ticket for each number, and get to choose where you want to deposit it. Decide what table(s) you’d like items from, and place your tickets in the corresponding tables’ pots.
  • When the event begins, volunteers will be stationed at each table to pull a series of tickets out of the pots. Those with the winning tickets from that table get to select an item from that table. This process continues from table to table until there are no more items or time runs out.

We look forward to seeing you there for this annual event.
