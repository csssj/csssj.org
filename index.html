Welcome, Visitors!

The Cactus and Succulent Society of San Jose (CSSSJ) welcomes you to our website.

The CSSSJ is a diverse group of people with a mutual interest in and fascination for cacti and other succulent plants.

Succulent plants are among the most bizarre, beautiful, and varied in the entire plant kingdom. If you share our interest in these fascinating and unique plants, or just want to learn more, we invite you to join us at our meetings and have some fun.

If you’d like to become a member, you can do so for $15 a person, or $20 a couple. See the Membership Page for additional details.

Club Meeting

Sunday, May 7th: Field Trip!

There are many fascinating sights on the Stanford University campus, but a relatively hidden spot is the Stanford Arizona Garden. It is located on the south side of the mausoleum off Quarry Road between Campus Drive and Arboretum Road. It was designed for Jane and Leland Stanford by landscape architect Rudolf Ulrich between 1881 and 1883. After suffering some neglect for a while, it had been neglected from the 1920s until recently. For several years, the Stanford grounds keeping staff and local cacti enthusiasts (including members of our club) have been restoring the garden to its former glory.

The Arizona Garden has a wide selection of cacti and succulents, something that everyone will surely appreciate. If you have considered getting rid of your lawn, it will provide plenty of inspiration for your project!

We encourage carpooling, and suggest parking near the Cantor Arts Center at Stanford University, then walking to the garden: please make sure to allow enough time for both, and see the directions below for the recommended path. Members should arrive at the garden by 12:30 pm. Our guide will be Julie Cain, the Historic Preservation Planner for Heritage Services at Stanford University. She will lead a tour of the garden starting at 12:45 pm.
